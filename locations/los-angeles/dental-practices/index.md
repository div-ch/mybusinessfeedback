## Why structured feedback matters for *dental practices in Los Angeles*

Los Angeles patients measure dental experiences in terms of time: how long the drive took, how many minutes they waited past their appointment slot, and whether the treatment itself was handled efficiently. A patient who battled 45 minutes of traffic to reach your Westside practice and then sat in the waiting room for 15 minutes has already formed an impression before the hygienist calls their name. Structured feedback captures that time-based frustration while it is specific enough to address, rather than after it has generalized into a vague sense that your practice is not worth the commute.

The gap between what the patient expected from a veneer consultation and what they experienced in the chair is where satisfaction judgments are formed.
